Earl Grey Cheesecake Cookies

Description

Originally, I followed a Red Velvet Cheesecake Cookies recipe from Ash Baber.

I adjusted the recipe to suit the earl grey flavour and it is so good. Especially, I love the addition of walnuts, unless you have nut allergy. Also, I use the loose tea leaves from the earl grey teabags.

Ingredients

Yields around 12 cookies

Cream Cheese Filling

  • 200g cream cheese
  • 40g sugar
  • 2 teaspoons corn flour
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 8g earl grey tea leaves

Cookie Dough

  • 240g unsalted butter, cold, cut into small cubes
  • 120g sugar
  • 240g soft light brown sugar
  • 4 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 520g all-purpose flour
  • 40g corn flour
  • 24g earl grey tea leaves
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 240g walnuts, chopped

Steps

* Prepare the cream cheese filling a few hours

    Cream Cheese Filling

  1. In a bowl, add all the ingredients for the filling and mix together until smooth.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon sized dollops of this filling onto a plate or tray, you want 12 dollops. Place these into your freezer for a few hours.

    3. Cookie Dough

  3. In a large bowl, add the cold butter, both sugars and the vanilla. Beat these together for 5 minutes.
  4. Add the eggs and egg yolks. Mix until combined.
  5. In a separate bowl, mix together the all-purpose flour, corn flour, earl grey tea leaves, baking powder and salt.
  6. Add this into your other bowl, along with the walnuts. Mix just until a dough forms and you can no longer see any flour.
  7. Get 120g of this dough and flatten it out. Add a piece of your frozen filling to the centre of this, then roll the cookie dough into a ball, making sure the filling is fully covered.
  8. Place this onto a plate or tray, then repeat until you have used all of your cookie dough.
  9. Leave these cookie dough balls in your freezer for at least 1 hour.

    10. Baking

  10. About 20 minutes before you are going to bake the cookies, pre-heat your oven to 220 C (428 F). Also line a large baking tray with some baking paper.
  11. Place your frozen cookie dough balls onto this tray, making sure to leave gaps between them so they have room to spread.
  12. Bake for around 18-20 minutes.
  13. Leave them to cool on the tray for about 15-20 minutes to set up. Then after, you can enjoy!
